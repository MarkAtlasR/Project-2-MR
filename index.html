SEPECAT JAGUAR T.2/T.4 FROM AIRFIX

AS SOON as Airfix’s 1/48 SEPECAT Jaguar GR.1/GR.1A was seen to have a separate nose section (and the inclusion of a second pilot figure), there was huge anticipation regarding a two-seat variant. The company has duly obliged with a SEPECAT Jaguar T.2 & T.4 (A11011), which features three markings schemes.

On opening the sturdy box you’ll find 13 styrene runners, ten of which are common with the initial GR.1/GR.1A boxing (see AMW171, February 2025 for a full build). Three new frames provide the four-piece cockpit glazing and extra head-up display combiner panels, plus the two-seat nose, an extra ejection seat (with a choice of early/late styles) and new cockpit tub with separate side consoles. In addition, several underwing items from the initial kit are employed here, such as the blanking plate on the right-hand cannon fairing (the T.2/T.4 has just a single 30mm Aden cannon, on the left-hand side) and the GPS antenna ‘bump’ on the fuselage spine for the T.4 airframes.

The larger cockpit area is arguably even more impressive than the single-seater, thanks to the capacious glazing, allowing the modeller to pose it either open or closed. In addition, there’s a choice between early-style instrument panels which feature a Projected Map Display (PMD) or the modernised configuration with a 6in x 8in Multi-Purpose Colour Display (MPCD). As before, though, there’s a choice of seats, but this time you get two examples each of the Martin-Baker Mk.9B Mk.2 (T.2) and Mk.9B Mk.3 (T.4). Other neat inclusions are the different control column types and a commendably fine pitot. In common with the single-seater, there’s a pair of underwing drop tanks. As before, the wing flaps and slats can be posed up or down.

Three schemes are included in this new boxing; the first is standard wraparound disruptive Dark Green and Dark Sea Grey, while the second dazzles with the Aeroplane & Armament Experimental Establishment’s (A&AEE) red-white-blue ‘raspberry ripple’ scheme. The final option is a contrast in styles, featuring the Jag’s final two-tone Dark Camouflage Grey-Dark Sea Grey camouflage, plus dual 80th anniversary of Cosford and 100th Anniversary of 238 Squadron’s formation markings. The choices are: T.2A, XX847/X, 41(F) Squadron, RAF Coltishall, July 1992, T.2, XX145, Empire Test Pilots’ School, A&AEE, Boscombe Down, 1992 ,T.4, XX835, 238 Squadron (ex-6 Squadron airframe), No.1 School of Technical Training, RAF Cosford, 2018 The well-printed decal sheet also supplies copious stencilling… essentially you get three types (camouflage, trials and low-vis), so you’ll have plenty of spares. Airfix’s 1/48 SEPECAT Jaguar T.2/T.4 is due for release shortly and will retail for £59.99
